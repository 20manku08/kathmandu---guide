Essential Tips for Visitors

Practical information to help you navigate your journey through the historic valley safely and respectfully.

🙏 Etiquette

Temple Respect

Always remove your shoes before entering religious sanctuaries. Walk clockwise around the stupas and shrines, and ensure your shoulders and knees are covered respectfully.

🚖 Transport

Local Commuting

Pathao, InDrive, and Yango are the easiest ride-sharing apps to use inside the valley. For a local experience, you can try out the local micro-buses or Safa Tempos.

☀️ Season

Autumn Clarity

The premium months to explore are between OCtober and December. The monsoon rains completely clear out the atmosphere, offering flawless views of the surrounding Himalayas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to drink the tap water in Kathmandu?

No, tap water is not safe for drinking. Stick strictly to bottled mineral water, which is cheap and widely available at every local shop.

Where can I get a local tourist SIM card?

You can easily get an Ncell or Nepal Telecom (NTC) SIM card right at Tribhuvan International Airport or at local telecom stalls using your passport copy and a photo.
