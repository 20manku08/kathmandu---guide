Sacred and Royal Landmarks

Take a digital walk through the three most crucial historical layouts points in Kathmandu Valley.

01

Kathamndu Durbar Square (Basantapur)

This plaza marks the royal headquarters of the Malla and Shah dynasties. It is a highly dense compound of open courtyards, stone steps, and towering multi-tiered pagoda temples. The complex houses the beautiful multi-windowed palace of the Kumari-the living human goddess of Nepal-where visitors can view incredible , hand-carved black wood frameworks.

02

Boudhanath Stupa

As one of the single largest spherical stupas across the entire globe, Boudhanath stands as the central beacon of Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal. The massive white dome symbolizes the the earth, while the square tower above displays the all-seeing eyesof the Buddha painted on all compass directions. Monks and travellers walk clockwise around the base, spinning prayer wheels.

03

Pashupatinath Temple Complex

Located right along the banks of the sacred river Bagmati , this expensive temple layout represents one of the most vital spiritual destination for Hindus across the planet. Dedicated to Lord Shiva in his form as Pashupati (Lord of Animals), the main structure features a stricking dual-tiered roof constructed from pure gold plate and thick silver-embossed doors.
