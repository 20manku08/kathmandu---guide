The Tastes of Kathmandu

Kathmandu's food scene is a mix of ancient Newari cultural recipes and hyper-popular modern street food items.

Steaming Plates of Momo

Local Staple

The definitve King of street food in Nepal. These are delicate, thin flour wrappers packed tightly with finely minced meat or mixed vegetables seasoned with ginger, garlic, and corainder. They are steamed inside inside metal pots and served piping hot alongside a rich, spicy fluid achar made from roasted tomatoes, sesame seeds, and chilies.

Authentic Newari Samay Baji

Feast Platter

A ceremonial, highly structured food layout served during major local festivals. The base layer is made of crispy Baji (beaten flakes of rice). Arranged cleanly around it are smoky buffalo Choila meat, spicy spiced potato-bamboo shoot soup (Alu Tama), roasted black soy beans, ginger paste, fried lentil patties called Bara, and a boiled egg.

JuJu Dhau (The King Yogurt)

Sweet Dessert

Originating directly from the historical neighoring city of Bhaktapur within the valley, JuJu Dhau literally translates to the "King of Yogurts". It is prepared using rich, fresh buffalo milk boiled in large cauldrons, sweetened with natural spices, and fremented inside porous clay pots that absorb excess water, leaving a thick, custard-like texture.
